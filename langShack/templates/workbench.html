"Papahana" means "workbench" in the Hawaiian language (ʻŌlelo hawaiʻi). The word "papahana" derives from Hawaiian's 'papa' - 'to write', and 'hana' - 'to work, do, make'.


This workbench provides a toolbelt for any linguist to analyze rare/endangered languages in depth. Some of these custom tools include
  • Comparing wordlists for similarities by CSV file
  • Studying the history and evolution of a language through a custom corpus
  • Inputting custom rule-based grammar patterns
  • Scanning old documents to text for analysis
  • Custom word tagger and trainer
  • Graphs and other features to show linguistic data
